---
title: "How do I customize the welcome email?"
category: "Email & Text"
---

The welcome email is what new members see first when you approve their application. It sets the tone — a warm, specific greeting beats a generic "Welcome!" every time.

### Where to find it

1. Click **Emails** in the top nav.
2. On the left, you'll see emails grouped by audience: **📥 To applicants**, **👋 To members**, **🛠️ To admins**.
3. Find the welcome email under **👋 To members** (it'll have "welcome" in the name).
4. Click it. The editor opens on the right.

### What you can change

- **Send this email when the trigger fires** — uncheck this if you want to disable the email entirely (rare).
- **Subject line** — keep it short. "Welcome to [Your Club]!" works well.
- **Body** — write a few short paragraphs. Cover:
  - When the pool opens and closes
  - Where to find the gate code or app
  - Who to email with questions
  - Any rules they should know upfront (no glass, etc.)

You can use the visual editor (Heading / Bold / Bullets / Link toolbar) or switch to **HTML** mode if you know HTML.

### The yellow chips (auto-fill bits)

Inside the body you'll see yellow chips with names like **Member's name**, **Club name**, or **Sign-in link**. These are placeholders. Each time the email is sent, Poolside swaps them for the real info. So one template with a "Member's name" chip becomes "Hi Linda," for Linda and "Hi Carlos," for Carlos.

To add one, click into the body where you want it, then click the chip you want from the **Click to insert a placeholder at your cursor** row below the editor. To remove one, click the chip in the body and press Delete like any other word.

**Don't remove the Sign-in link chip if your members rely on it.** It's how they get into the app from the email.

### Preview it before saving

The **Live preview** pane at the bottom updates as you type. The chips fill in with sample data so you can see what a real email will look like.

When you're happy, click **Save**. To go back to the original, click **Reset to default** (only shows when you've customized it).

> **Tip:** Boards that personalize this email see fewer "I can't sign in" calls because the instructions are clearer. Mention the gate code, hours, and a real human's name to email.
