---
title: "I just signed up — what should I do first?"
category: "Getting Started"
---

Welcome! Here's the short list every new club tackles in week one. None of these takes more than a few minutes.

### 1. Add a backup admin (most important — 1 minute)

If only one person can sign in to Poolside and that person loses their phone, the whole board is locked out. Click **Settings**, scroll to the **Club admins** card, and invite at least one other board member. (See the article "How do I add another admin?" if you need step-by-step.)

### 2. Set your membership tiers and dues amounts

Click **Members → Tiers**. A "tier" is a level your members can pick from when they apply — for example, **Family** ($400), **Single** ($250), **Senior** ($150). You can have just one tier if your club charges everyone the same. Set the prices to whatever your board decided, then click **Save tiers**.

### 3. Enter last year's members (or invite them to apply)

You have two options:

- **Have them apply themselves** (cleaner). Your public application link is `https://<your-slug>.poolsideapp.com/apply.html` — you'll see your slug at the top of the **Dashboard** under your club name. Email that link to last year's families. They fill it in, you approve under **Members → Applications**.
- **Enter them yourself**. Go to **Members → Households** and click **+ Add household** to fill in each family by hand. Useful if you only have a paper roster from last year.

### 4. Send the season-opening dues reminder

Once your members are in the system, click **Members → Renewals** and send your first blast. (See "How do I send a dues reminder?" for details.)

### 5. (Optional) Turn on card payments

If you want members to pay dues by credit card instead of just check or Venmo, see "How do I switch to real card payments?" Skip this if your club has always done cash and checks — Poolside lets you keep doing exactly that.

### 6. Check the Status page

Click **Status** in the top nav. Anything yellow or red is something to fix. Green checks mean members can actually do that thing — sign in, pay, get email, and so on. It's a good way to spot anything that fell through the cracks.

> **Tip:** Don't try to do all six in one sitting. Most boards spread setup over a week. The order above is the right one — finish #1 today, even if you do nothing else.
